Through The Dresden State Art Collections / The Metropolitan Museum of Art Staff Exchange Program, curators have conducted research in their areas of expertise and together have studied the rich collections of paintings, drawings, and prints. A significant discovery was made by a Met curator who traveled to Dresden to research sixteenth- and seventeenth-century Italian drawings. During her time spent in the study room at the Staatliche Museen, she unearthed a drawing by the young Raphael that was subsequently published in Apollo Magazine and The Burlington Magazine.

Made possible by the Arnhold family in honor of Henry H. Arnhold.
